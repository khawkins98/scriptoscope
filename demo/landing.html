Paint at 1×, ship at 4×.

A pixel-art tool for designers who want their classic-Mac game icons to render correctly on a 5K display in 2026.

PixelDraw is a vector-aware bitmap editor. You draw at native pixel resolution; PixelDraw exports clean integer-scaled PNGs at 2×, 3×, 4×, and SVG fallback. Designed for game devs, retro-icon designers, and anyone shipping pixel art to modern displays.

macOS 12+ · Windows 11 · Linux · free trial · $29 license

Pixel-grid math

Every brush, every transform, every export is grid-snapped. No sub-pixel drift, ever. Your 24×24 ICN# stays exactly 24×24 after a hundred edits.

⤢

Integer export

One source, eight exports. PixelDraw writes 1×, 2×, 3×, 4×, 8× PNGs in one pass; the upscale is nearest-neighbor sample-and-hold, not bilinear blur.

Period palettes

Ships with Mac System 7 (8-bit), Mac OS 8 Platinum, Windows 3.1 (16-color), and BeOS R5 palettes. Snap any source to any palette in one click.
